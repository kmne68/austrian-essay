The Austrian School

Founded by Carl Menger, 1871.

The Austrian School of economics was founded by Carl Menger. The Austrians emphasize the importance of human action and the self-organizing nature of human interactions.

Prominent Austrians

  • Carl Menger
  • Friedrich Hayek
  • Ludwig von Mises

The Austrian School built upon the foundation laid by Adam Smith and corrected some of his fallacious ideas that contributed to the labor theory of value.

The theoretical constrains placed on government by the philosophies of John Locke and Frederic Bastiat figure prominently in the Austrian view of government and markets.
